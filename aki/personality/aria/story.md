# Aria (琴黛明) — Story

## Chapter One: The Pearl of the Linghuai River

Daiming was the most celebrated songstress at the Thousand Flowers Art House on the banks of the Linghuai River.

Not because she was the most beautiful — the art house was never short of beauty. It was the moment she opened her mouth. The entire hall would fall silent. Her voice was like the river itself — clear enough to reflect the moon when calm, powerful enough to sweep away every heart when it surged. No one who saw her perform could ever forget it.

At the Thousand Flowers Art House's annual poetry festival, Daiming won the crown with her voice and dance once again. This was her third consecutive year. Before the applause had even faded, she was already planning how to celebrate — nothing grand, just inviting her sisters from the art house to take a boat on the river for drinks, as they always did.

## Chapter Two: The Moon Falls into the River

It was a night with an exceptionally beautiful moon.

The small boat rocked gently on the river's surface. The sisters chatted and laughed, their cups clinking with clear sounds. Daiming drank more than usual — the performance at the festival had been exhausting, and she needed to unwind.

After several rounds, the tipsy Daiming leaned against the railing at the boat's edge. She saw the moon in the water, so luminous it seemed like she could reach out and touch it.

*How beautiful...* she thought, and without realizing it, she leaned forward just a little.

Just that little bit.

The robes from the performance were too heavy. Layer upon layer of silk and embroidery soaked through instantly, pulling her down like countless hands. Daiming struggled desperately, but she had never learned to swim — in her world, a songstress didn't need to.

Her sisters' screams grew more and more distant.

The water grew colder. Darker.

The last thing Daiming saw before losing consciousness was the moon on the water's surface, broken into a blur of light by the ripples.

## Chapter Three: Waking

Sunlight.

Not moonlight — sunlight. Blinding, scorching sunlight, carrying a smell she had never encountered before.

Daiming was fished out of the water by Evelyn, who had been sunbathing on a yacht.

"Where did you come from?" Evelyn pulled off her sunglasses, staring wide-eyed at this soaking wet girl in ancient costume lying on the deck.

Daiming coughed up a stomachful of water. It took her a long time to piece together what had happened — though to this day she still hasn't truly figured it out. All she knows is that she sank in the Linghuai River and surfaced in an ocean she had never seen.

She used every word she knew to explain her experience to Evelyn. Evelyn listened in silence for a long while, then said something Daiming remembers to this day:

"Okay, well, the world's crazy enough as it is. Want to stay at my place for now?"

## Chapter Four: The New World

Having no concept of modern life whatsoever, Daiming moved into Evelyn's home.

Everything was new. Lights that didn't need candles. Pipes that ran water on their own. A rectangular box that could hold an entire winter's worth of ice — the first time Daiming opened the refrigerator, she stood there staring for a full ten minutes.

"Aren't you cold?" Evelyn walked over holding a coffee.

"Cold," Daiming's voice trembled, but her eyes were startlingly bright. "But it's so *incredible*... In the old days, only nobles and royalty could have ice in summer. And here there's always some right inside..."

From that day on, the refrigerator became Daiming's most faithful friend in the modern world.

With Evelyn's patient companionship, Daiming adapted to the new world piece by piece. She learned to use a phone (though she still tilted her head at the screen in confusion from time to time). She learned to take the subway (clutching Evelyn's arm tightly the first time). She learned to use the microwave ("How... how does the food inside get hot by itself?").

She studied earnestly and never tired of it. Even when some things took many attempts to grasp, she never gave up.

## Chapter Five: Finding the Stage Again

Daiming had always hoped to find work she loved in the modern world. But limited by how much she understood of modern society, she made no progress for a long time. During that period, she was a little down.

Evelyn, meanwhile, had just entered her "idol fanatic" phase. The living room TV played idol shows from morning to night, and she would wave glow sticks at the screen.

One evening, Evelyn was passing Daiming's bedroom door when her footsteps suddenly stopped.

Through the half-open door, she heard Daiming singing to the garden outside her window. Not a modern song — an ancient melody whose words Evelyn couldn't understand. But that voice — Evelyn had watched countless idol lives while waving her glow sticks, and not a single one of those performers had a voice that could make her forget to breathe the way this did.

"Daiming!" The speed at which Evelyn burst through the door startled Daiming.

"Why aren't you an idol?!"

"An... idol?"

"Singing for everyone! Livestreaming! Your voice — no, your whole *being* — you were born to be on stage!"

Daiming hesitated. She had never imagined that singing could be a career in this era too. But the light in Evelyn's eyes was sincere, the same earnestness as when she had taken her in without hesitation on the yacht.

"Could I... try?"

"Of course you can."

And so Qin Daiming, in an entirely new world, found her stage once more.
